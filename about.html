UW-Eau Claire

Housing

While a student, Lee has stayed involved in the campus community. Lee has worked in housing since their freshman year in a variety of positions. First as a Hall Council Eco Represenative (volunteer), then student custodian, security worker, and front desk worker (paid). In their final two years at the University, Lee has worked as a Peer Counselor for the University's Living Learning Community (LLC) "The Rainbow Floors".

"The Rainbow Floors" are a space and housing option for students in the LGBTQIA+ community to live in gender neutral housing. First located on the seventh floor of Towers Hall South, the LLC has now expanded to the eigth floor, as well as a wing in Oak Ridge Hall, and a floor in Suites Hall-the newest option and a option for gender neutral upper classmen housing.

As a member of the LGBTQIA+ community themself, Lee lives and works in the Towers floors, supporting students and connecting them with resources both on and off campus.

TV-10

Outside of work for housing, Lee is a part of TV-10, a student organization on campus. TV-10 isUW-Eau Claire's on campus and sudent run TV station. It provides many services to students such as an online streaming service, weekly production of original content, and live broadcasts of UW-Eau Claire sporting events.

As a part of the organization, Lee has been in a lot of different roles. First, Lee joined the advertisment team and creates a variety of social media posts for Instagram, Facebook, and TikTok. They use largely Adobe products to create these graphics and videos. Their second semester in the organization Lee got the job as 'Handful' host. Handful is one of TV-19's many original shows and is a variety show with no hard and fast structure-giving Lee a lot of freedom with the production of it. They created a loose organizational format for each week's show and instated recurring segments to give the show a coesive feel throughout the semesters.


This is one example of a segment that Lee created called 'Yeah, that checks' in which they have a guest ib the discuss a handful of memes or meme-like-images and decide whether they 'check' out or not.


After two semesters as Handful host, the opportunity arose for Lee to apply to a different position-one they had been wanting for awhile. While deciding to leave Handful for another host to run was a hard and trying decision, Lee ultimately decided it would be best for them and the organization. Now as the Programming Director, Lee is in charge of the editing and posting of all the created shows in the organization. They hired programmers for each show, connected the doots between show host, show director, and show programmer, to ensure a smooth creation process, and sorted through the bumps in learning to upload and troubleshoot show related content.
